About Me

My name is Ben DeAngelis and I'm a recent graduate from NC State's Game Development Program. I see video games as the ultimate form of artwork, a medium that combines visuals, music, and narrative together to create something truly emotional. I've spent much of my life playing these masterpieces, with my favorite games being narrative-based like Assassin's Creed 2, the Mass Effect Trilogy, and Ori and the Blind Forest.

In my coursework I have explored everything from building a game engine from scratch to devleoping a behavior tree to creating a full-sized Unity game with a small team of classmates. In my free time, I've focused myself on narrative and rules design, writing short stories and creating homebrew content for my D&D game.

Game Development

Shadow's Bounty

Platform(s): Windows, Mac
Engine and tools: Unity, Probuilder, Photon Bolt
Team size: 6
Position: Art Team Lead, Level Design Team Lead, Narrative Designer, Trailer Editor
My contribution: I built and lit the first map from scratch, wrote the majority of the lore and edited the trailer.

A four-player free-for-all stealth action game where the objective is to avoid the guards and gather more treasure than your opponents. The game features two maps, one designed to showcase stealth and the other to showcase map traversal. Players can purchase upgrades to help protect against the guards or traverse the map more quickly.

Narrative Design

The Cross

A science fiction story about Garrett, a low-level military officer in a post apocalyptic world where society has rebuilt itself around a religious cult. The story follows Garrett as he prepares for his final test before promotion. Offers an exploration into the dangers of blind loyalty, societal indoctrination and objective truth versus subjective true.

Other Projects

AI Behavior Tree

A combined implementation of a behavior tree and a pathfinding algorithm using Processing 3. The AI character "The Knight" is given the task of defeating the evil ram, Ramses, by collecting and crafting items and weapons. Only some weapons can actually beat Ramses, and only some items are available at the start of the simulation (determined by a pre-created JSON file). Using a behavior tree and A-star, the AI determines what item it needs to get closer to the goal and then finds its way to the area it can get that item.
